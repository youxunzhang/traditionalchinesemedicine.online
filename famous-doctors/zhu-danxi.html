Zhu Danxi

Nourish yin, restrain fire

Yuan dynasty · 1281–1358 CE

Biography

Zhu Danxi, also known as Zhu Zhenheng, observed that the stresses of literati life—late nights, rich food, emotional intensity—tended to deplete yin and stir internal fire. He argued that most people had “yang in surplus and yin in deficiency.”

His prescriptions therefore emphasise moistening, cooling, and directing the ministerial fire downward.

Clinical Focus

Women’s health, emotional regulation, and chronic inflammatory conditions marked by yin deficiency heat.

Key Contributions

  • Promoted the idea that quiet cultivation and diet are essential allies to herbal therapy.
  • Created formulas such as Zhi Bai Di Huang Wan to clear heat while nourishing fluids.
  • Balanced the tonifying strategies of his contemporaries with gentle restraint.

Recommended Reading

  • Gezhi Yulun (Words of Discourse on Knowledge)

Legacy Note

Zhu’s reminder that lifestyle shapes our internal fire resonates today. His yin-nourishing formulas remain central for perimenopausal transition, stress-related insomnia, and skin flare-ups.
