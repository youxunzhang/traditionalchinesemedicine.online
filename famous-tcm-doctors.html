Influential TCM Practitioners

From legendary physicians of antiquity to twentieth-century reformers, these practitioners helped shape the art of pattern differentiation and herbal prescribing.

Zhang Zhongjing

Eastern Han · ca. 150–219 CE

The “Sage of Medicine” whose Shang Han Lun and Jinkui Yaolue codified the six conformations and the idea that each pattern deserves its own formula.

Read profile→

Sun Simiao

Tang dynasty · 581–682 CE

Known as the “King of Medicine,” Sun championed compassionate ethics, preventive care, and extensive formularies in his Essential Prescriptions Worth a Thousand Gold.

Read profile→

Li Shizhen

Ming dynasty · 1518–1593 CE

A meticulous field researcher whose Compendium of Materia Medica corrected errors and added hundreds of herbs, becoming the cornerstone of herbal scholarship.

Read profile→

Hua Tuo

Late Eastern Han · ca. 145–208 CE

Surgical pioneer and creator of the Five Animal Frolics exercises, Hua Tuo is celebrated for anaesthetic innovation and agile, whole-body training.

Read profile→

Bian Que

Warring States · ca. 407–310 BCE

A master diagnostician whose four examinations—looking, listening, asking, and palpating—became the standard for identifying patterns before they progress.

Read profile→

Wang Shuhe

Western Jin · ca. 210–286 CE

Compiled the Pulse Classic and preserved Zhang Zhongjing’s formulas, cementing pulse diagnosis as a diagnostic art.

Read profile→

Li Dongyuan

Jin dynasty · 1180–1251 CE

Founder of the Spleen and Stomach school who emphasised nourishing earth to treat chronic fatigue, digestive issues, and lingering dampness.

Read profile→

Zhu Danxi

Yuan dynasty · 1281–1358 CE

A champion of nourishing yin and clearing ministerial fire, offering balance to the hotter prescriptions of his era.

Read profile→

Ye Tianshi

Qing dynasty · 1667–1746 CE

Warm disease specialist who articulated the defensive–qi–nutrient–blood model, shaping how febrile illnesses are treated today.

Read profile→

Wu Jutong

Qing dynasty · 1758–1836 CE

Successor to the warm disease lineage whose Wen Bing Tiao Bian provides precise herbal strategies for progressive heat disorders.

Read profile→

Qi Bo

Mythic antiquity

The legendary imperial physician in the Huangdi Neijing, offering dialogues on seasonal living, meridian theory, and preventive care.

Read profile→

Ni Hai-Xia

Contemporary · 1954–2012 CE

An inspirational teacher who brought classical formulas to English-speaking audiences, emphasising astronomy, channel theory, and compassionate clinical practice.
