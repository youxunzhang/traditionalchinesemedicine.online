Bian Que

Master of the four examinations

Warring States · ca. 407–310 BCE

Biography

Bian Que, born Qin Yueren, is often credited as the ancestor of Chinese medicine. Legends recount his ability to see subtle imbalances before they erupted into illness, offering the famous warning to the ruler of Cai that disease must be treated while still superficial.

Though mythic in tone, these stories highlight the value TCM places on early detection and preventive care.

Clinical Focus

Holistic diagnosis through looking, listening, questioning, and palpating, along with the use of acupuncture and herbal intervention.

Key Contributions

  • Popularised the four diagnostic pillars that remain central to clinical encounters.
  • Symbolises the ethic of “treating before disease arises.”
  • Expanded the reach of acupuncture and pulse diagnosis in early China.

Legacy Note

Bian Que’s legend reminds us that attentive observation can redirect the course of illness. His name has become synonymous with trustworthy, perceptive care.
