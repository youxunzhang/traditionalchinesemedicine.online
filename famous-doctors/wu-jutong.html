Wu Jutong

Refiner of warm disease treatment

Qing dynasty · 1758–1836 CE

Biography

Wu Jutong studied under Ye Tianshi’s disciples and continued to document epidemic diseases affecting the lower Yangzi region. His notes highlight how heat toxins can lodge in the membrane source, lingering even after fevers fade.

He organised these insights into the Wen Bing Tiao Bian, a practical manual for clinicians facing rapidly changing symptoms.

Clinical Focus

Later-stage warm diseases, fluid damage, and complications affecting the pericardium and liver.

Key Contributions

  • Expanded the defensive–qi–nutrient–blood model with clear formula strategies at each stage.
  • Emphasised aromatic, cooling herbs to resolve turbidity lodged between the exterior and interior.
  • Linked lingering pathogens with modern ideas about post-viral syndromes.

Recommended Reading

  • Wen Bing Tiao Bian (Systematic Differentiation of Warm Diseases)

Legacy Note

Wu’s formulations—such as Yin Qiao San and Qing Ying Tang—remain staples in clinics worldwide. His work bridges classical insights with the challenges of emerging infectious disease.
