Qi Bo

Advisor to the Yellow Emperor

Mythic antiquity

Biography

Qi Bo appears in the Huangdi Neijing as the wise minister answering the Yellow Emperor’s questions about physiology, pathology, and longevity. Though a mythical figure, his teachings represent collective wisdom gathered from early physicians.

The dialogues cover seasonal living, the functions of the five zang and six fu, meridians, and the importance of harmonising with nature.

Clinical Focus

Holistic cosmology, preventive health, and the theoretical bedrock of Traditional Chinese Medicine.

Key Contributions

  • Articulated yin-yang and five phase theory as applied to human health.
  • Explained the circulation of qi and blood through the twelve primary channels.
  • Linked emotions, seasons, and lifestyle to the vitality of each organ system.

Legacy Note

Qi Bo symbolises the roots of TCM thinking. Practitioners turn to his dialogues whenever they need to reconnect clinical technique with cosmological insight.
