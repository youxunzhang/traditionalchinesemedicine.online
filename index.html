Food as Medicine

Quick Nutrition Snapshots

Blend whole grains, legumes, and seasonal produce into everyday meals. You’ll capture the fibre and phytonutrients celebrated by modern nutrition while honouring the TCM idea that healing begins in the kitchen.

Oat Congee

Dietary fibre 3.5 g · Plant protein 5.9 g · Rich in beta-glucan

Moistens dryness, gently supports the stomach, and steadies post-meal blood sugar. Pair with jujube or goji for extra qi and blood nourishment and enjoy warm in the morning to keep digestion moving.

Chinese Yam & Job’s Tears Porridge

Complex carbs · Protein 4.1 g · Potassium & B vitamins

Chinese yam fortifies the spleen and boosts qi, while Job’s tears transform dampness. Together they replenish energy and drain heaviness—ideal for those feeling sluggish or puffy.

Snow Fungus & Lotus Seed Dessert

Polysaccharides · Fibre · Plant calcium & potassium

Snow fungus nourishes lung yin and fluids, lotus seeds calm the spirit, and a sprinkle of goji berries supports liver and kidney balance. A comforting evening dessert that doesn’t create excess heat.

Black Sesame Elixir

Unsaturated fats · Calcium 780 mg · Vitamin E

Black sesame seeds replenish liver and kidney yin, promote glossy hair, and ease dryness. Blend with walnut powder to sharpen memory and nourish bones—excellent for students and elders alike.

Essential Reading

Curated classical texts and modern guides that reveal how Traditional Chinese Medicine thinks, diagnoses, and heals.

Huangdi Neijing

Meridians · Viscera · Foundational theory

The definitive source on yin-yang dynamics, five phases, organ systems, and pattern differentiation—essential for grasping TCM’s holistic worldview.

Shang Han Lun

Six conformations · Classical formulas

Zhang Zhongjing’s clinical masterpiece detailing how pathogenic cold moves through the body and how targeted formulas restore balance.

Jinkui Yaolue

Internal medicine · Formula differentiation

A companion to the Shang Han Lun covering chronic and organ-related disorders. Each formula is matched to precise signs, guiding nuanced treatment.

Shennong Bencao Jing

Materia medica origins · Herbal energetics

The earliest pharmacopoeia, classifying 365 herbs into superior, moderate, and regular grades and establishing the language of taste, temperature, and channel entry.

Nanjing

Meridians & pulse · Q&A insights

Forty-nine classic questions that clarify difficult passages from the Inner Canon, offering practitioners guidance on pulse interpretation and channel theory.

Wen Bing Tiao Bian

Defensive-qi-nutrient-blood · Warm pathogen theory

Wu Jutong maps how heat pathogens progress from the exterior to deeper levels, offering modern relevance for febrile and viral conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Honest answers to the questions we hear most about living and healing with Traditional Chinese Medicine.

How long does it take to feel results from herbal therapy?

Everyone’s constitution responds differently. Acute concerns may ease within one to two weeks, while deeper imbalances often require one to three months of consistent care. We reassess at each follow-up and adjust formulas to meet your progress.

Are there side effects with Chinese herbal medicine?

When prescribed appropriately, herbs are gentle and balanced. We screen for allergies, drug interactions, and quality sourcing. Follow the recommended dosage, observe how you feel, and contact us immediately if anything feels off so we can fine-tune.

How do you decide which herbs are right for me?

We begin with a thorough TCM intake—observing tongue, pulse, lifestyle, and emotions. Patterns of disharmony guide the formula. As your body shifts, we adjust the ratio of herbs to keep the treatment specific and effective.

Do you offer wholesale sourcing for clinics?

Yes. Licensed practitioners and pharmacies can partner with us for sustainably grown raw herbs and granules. Minimum orders apply so we can maintain freshness. Reach out with your credentials and we will arrange a sourcing plan.

What’s the difference between decoctions and convenient tea sachets?

Traditional decoctions are simmered for 30–45 minutes and offer the richest potency. Sachets provide pre-measured herbs you can steep on busy days. We help you combine both approaches so convenience never sacrifices therapeutic depth.

How do you ensure rare herbs are authentic?

We work with growers who use third-party lab testing and traditional organoleptic checks. Certificates of analysis accompany premium botanicals, and our team inspects appearance, aroma, and texture before anything reaches your hands.

Can TCM help with weight management safely?

Rather than chasing quick fixes, we regulate digestion, transform dampness, and calm stress-driven cravings. Personalised herbs pair with meal rhythm and movement strategies so weight shifts feel steady and supportive.

How does TCM approach beauty and skin health?

Radiant skin starts within. We use herbs and food therapy to nourish blood, move qi, and clear heat. Topical masks, botanical steams, and facial gua sha support circulation from the outside for a naturally luminous glow.
