Li Shizhen

Botanical encyclopedist

Ming dynasty · 1518–1593 CE

Biography

Li Shizhen travelled extensively across China’s mountains and wetlands, tasting and cataloguing plants alongside farmers, hunters, and fishermen. Dissatisfied with errors that had crept into medical texts, he verified each entry through meticulous observation.

His three-decade project culminated in the Bencao Gangmu, an illustrated encyclopedia that ties botanical description, medicinal properties, and cultural usage together.

Clinical Focus

Materia medica research, accurate identification of herbs, and integrating food therapy with therapeutic prescriptions.

Key Contributions

  • Documented 1,892 substances, many with notes on harvesting, processing, and toxicity.
  • Introduced hundreds of corrections and cross-references to earlier texts.
  • Showed how climate, geography, and preparation change an herb’s potency.

Recommended Reading

  • Bencao Gangmu (Compendium of Materia Medica)

Legacy Note

Li’s fieldwork inspires today’s herbalists to respect both scientific testing and community wisdom. His insistence on accuracy underpins modern quality-control standards.
