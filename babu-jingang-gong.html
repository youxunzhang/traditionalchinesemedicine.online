Ba Bu Jingang Gong

Also known as the Eight Sections of the Vajra, this routine refines strength, tendon elasticity, and inner stillness. It builds on the foundation of the Eight Brocades with deeper spirals and intent.

  • Progressive training drills that emphasise rooting and whole-body power.
  • Meditation prompts for each section to stabilise the mind.
  • Safety checkpoints to keep joints open and breath unstrained.

Movement breakdown

Eight sections with intent

Each sequence weaves precise alignment with focused breathwork. Move deliberately, pausing between sections to absorb the sensations.

1 · Vajra Awakens the Spine

Raise and lower the arms in arcs while rooting the feet. Imagine the spine unfurling vertebra by vertebra.

  • Breath: inhale to lift, exhale to settle the weight into the heels.
  • Mindset: feel the lower dantian glow as the base of support.

2 · Vajra Lifts the Sky

Palms spiral overhead, elbows relaxed. The lifting sensation originates at the sacrum and travels upward.

  • Breath: gather on the inhale, release on the exhale.
  • Mindset: sense the body as a single column connecting earth and sky.

3 · Vajra Draws the Bow

Feet plant wide; torso twists as the arms open diagonally. Engage the waist to lead the movement.

  • Breath: inhale as you open, exhale as the bow releases.
  • Mindset: direct intent past the fingertips, steady but powerful.

4 · Vajra Turns the Wheel

Hands describe a large circle in front of the body. The pelvis remains heavy while the upper body spirals.

  • Breath: smooth, unbroken; follow the hands with the breath.
  • Mindset: soften the chest and allow qi to flow through the girdle vessel.

5 · Vajra Presses the Mountain

Palms press outward as the knees bend. Maintain length in the spine and a lifted crown.

  • Breath: exhale with the press to engage the core.
  • Mindset: cultivate quiet strength, as if holding space for the body to expand.

6 · Vajra Polishes the Pillar

Alternating hands sweep along the spine, massaging the governor vessel. Coordinate with subtle flexion and extension.

  • Breath: match the sweep of the hands to inhaling up and exhaling down.
  • Mindset: imagine clearing obstructions so qi can rise smoothly.

7 · Vajra Offers the Cauldron

Arms cradle an invisible vessel forward, then draw it back to the dantian. Knees and hips move as one.

  • Breath: inhale to gather, exhale to present.
  • Mindset: nourish the heart and lungs with expansive intention.

8 · Vajra Returns to Stillness

Hands settle over the lower abdomen. Stand quietly, allowing micro-movements to ripple and fade.

  • Breath: natural, delicate, almost imperceptible.
  • Mindset: observe the body’s internal resonance without judgment.

Training blueprint

Build your practice

Adapt the routine to match your experience level and daily energy.

Foundation cycle

Three sessions per week · 25 minutes each

  • Warm-up with standing meditation and joint rotations.
  • Practise sections 1–4 with five breaths per posture.
  • Finish with a gentle seated breathing exercise.

Integration cycle

Four sessions per week · 35 minutes each

  • Add sections 5–8, emphasising smooth transitions.
  • Record observations in a practice journal.
  • Alternate evening and morning sessions to feel different energetic qualities.

Refinement cycle

Five sessions per week · 45 minutes each

  • Include silk-reeling drills between sections for tendon conditioning.
  • Introduce partner feedback or mirror work once a week.
  • Conclude with quiet standing to sense micro-circulation.
