Seasonal guidance

Spring · Wood element

Focus on lightly cooked greens, sprouts, and sour flavours. Sip chrysanthemum tea to cool rising yang.

Summer · Fire element

Hydrate with watermelon, cucumber, and mung bean soup. Keep cooking methods quick to preserve vitality.

Late Summer · Earth element

Support the spleen with millet, sweet potato, and adzuki bean soups. Avoid excessive cold drinks.

Autumn · Metal element

Moisten lung yin with pear, lily bulb, sesame, and honey. Add ginger to dispel wind chill.

Winter · Water element

Warm the kidneys with black beans, lamb broth, and cinnamon-spiced teas. Emphasise hearty stews.

Quick tips

Make every meal count

Small shifts can align your diet with TCM principles.

Balance temperature

Pair warming ingredients (ginger, cinnamon, lamb) with cooling ones (pear, cucumber, tofu) to suit your internal climate.

Favour whole grains

Switch refined carbs for millet, barley, or brown rice to stabilise qi and nourish the spleen.

Eat with the clock

Enjoy the largest meal at midday when digestive fire peaks; keep dinners lighter to support sleep.
