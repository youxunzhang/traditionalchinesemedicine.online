Li Dongyuan

Champion of spleen and stomach qi

Jin dynasty · 1180–1251 CE

Biography

Li Dongyuan, also known as Li Gao, practised during a time of war-induced famine and emotional strain. He observed that chronic fatigue, digestive weakness, and lingering dampness stemmed from depleted middle burner qi.

His school emphasises nourishing earth, raising clear yang, and gently dispersing damp-heat so that the body can recover its own momentum.

Clinical Focus

Strengthening digestion, treating chronic fatigue, and restoring the transformative power of qi through warm, sweet, and ascending herbs.

Key Contributions

  • Developed flagship formulas such as Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang and Shen Ling Bai Zhu San.
  • Linked emotional stress and overwork to spleen qi deficiency.
  • Balanced tonification with gentle clearing to prevent cloying stagnation.

Recommended Reading

  • Pi Wei Lun (Treatise on the Spleen and Stomach)

Legacy Note

Li’s insights guide many modern clinicians who treat burnout, digestive disorders, and long-haul recovery. His formulas remain staples for gently rebuilding resilience.
