Movement glossary

Eight forms, eight intentions

Each posture has a classical name and a targeted effect. Use the notes below to fine-tune your form.

1. Two Hands Hold up the Heavens

Stretch from soles to fingertips as you inhale. Imagine the spine elongating while the shoulders melt away from the ears. Exhale to let palms float down.

  • Focus: lengthen the spine and open the ribcage.
  • Tip: keep elbows soft; avoid locking the knees.

2. Drawing the Bow to Shoot the Hawk

Shift weight to the right, extend the left arm as though nocking an arrow, then reverse. Breathe in as you open, out as you release.

  • Focus: stimulate the lung and heart channels.
  • Tip: keep hips level; let the gaze follow the imaginary arrow.

3. Separate Heaven and Earth

One hand lifts up while the other presses down. Alternate sides with gentle spirals through the torso.

  • Focus: harmonise digestion and ease abdominal tension.
  • Tip: imagine hands moving through water to keep resistance light.

4. Wise Owl Gazes Backward

Turn the head slowly from side to side while maintaining a tall posture. Coordinate with steady breathing.

  • Focus: release neck and shoulder tension.
  • Tip: pivot from the upper back rather than wrenching the neck.

5. Sway the Head and Shake the Tail

Shift into a wide stance, circle the torso, and sway the hips to disperse heat from the heart.

  • Focus: soothe irritability and support digestion.
  • Tip: keep movements smooth and circular, not jerky.

6. Two Hands Hold the Feet

Fold forward from the hips, sweeping the hands along the legs before rising. Coordinate the bend with a relaxed exhale.

  • Focus: release the back line of the body; massage the kidneys.
  • Tip: bend knees slightly if hamstrings feel tight.

7. Clench Fists with Fiery Eyes

Sink into a horse stance, punch forward with alternating fists, and emit a steady breath to release inner strength.

  • Focus: strengthen legs and fortify protective qi.
  • Tip: keep shoulders dropped and wrists aligned.

8. Bounce on the Heels to Prevent Illness

Rise onto the balls of the feet and gently drop the heels, sending soft vibrations through the body.

  • Focus: stimulate lymphatic flow and awaken the kidney channel.
  • Tip: land softly; imagine waves rippling through the fascia.

Frequently asked questions

Clear guidance to help you build a steady, enjoyable routine.

How often should I practise the Eight Brocades?

Five to six sessions per week build momentum quickly. Even three shorter sessions keep the body open and responsive—consistency matters more than intensity.

Can I combine Eight Brocades with strength or cardio training?

Absolutely. Practise Ba Duan Jin first to warm the joints, then move into strength or cardio work. Alternatively, use it as a cool-down to transition into rest.

What should I focus on if I’m practising at night?

Slow down the tempo, lengthen the exhalations, and emphasise postures three through six. Finish with a gentle seated meditation to anchor the calm.
